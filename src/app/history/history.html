Tarot Origins

The Story Behind Tarot Reading

Tarot reading began as a deck of painted playing cards in 15th-century Europe. Over centuries, those cards became a language of symbols, myth, and inner reflection. Today, tarot is a living practice that combines archetypal imagery with personal intuition.

Each spread forms its own story. The order and position of the cards create a meaningful journey that can illuminate choices, reveal hidden patterns, and help readers connect with deeper guidance.

✨

Ancient Symbolism

Early tarot decks were rich with allegory and astrology. The Major Arcana describe human growth, while the Minor Arcana speak to everyday experience. The imagery invites every reader into a shared mythic landscape.

🪞

Reading Meaning

A tarot reading is less about fortune telling and more about reflection. Cards are mirrors that reveal possibilities and remind us of the energies already present in our lives.

🌙

Modern Practice

Contemporary tarot embraces personal intuition, meditation, and storytelling. Readers use spreads to ask questions, explore relationships, and gain perspective before choosing a path.
